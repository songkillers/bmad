# Acknowledgments and References

## Project Attribution

This project, "AI PINN - Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Groundwater Pollution Diffusion," was developed by **songkillers** as part of research into uncertainty quantification methods for environmental modeling applications.

## Methodological Foundations

### Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs)
This work builds upon the groundbreaking research in Physics-Informed Neural Networks introduced by:

- **Raissi, M., Perdikaris, P., & Karniadakis, G. E. (2019)**. "Physics-informed neural networks: A deep learning framework for solving forward and inverse problems involving nonlinear partial differential equations." *Journal of Computational Physics, 378*, 686-707.

### Monte Carlo Dropout for Uncertainty Quantification
The uncertainty quantification approach utilizes:

- **Gal, Y., & Ghahramani, Z. (2016)**. "Dropout as a Bayesian approximation: Representing model uncertainty in deep learning." *International conference on machine learning*, 1050-1059.

### Development Methodology
This project was developed using the BMAD (Brownfield Method for Agile Development) framework:

- **BMAD Framework**: An enterprise-grade agile development methodology for brownfield projects. The framework provides structured workflows for analysis, planning, implementation, and testing of complex software systems.

This research addresses critical challenges in:

- **Groundwater contamination modeling**: Predicting pollutant transport in subsurface environments
